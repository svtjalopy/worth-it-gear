What makes golf balls different from each other?

Golf balls differ in compression, spin rate, cover material, and layer count — all of which affect distance, feel, and control.

Which golf ball is best for beginners?

Beginner-friendly balls usually have low compression and a soft feel. They’re easier to hit and more forgiving.

What does compression mean?

Compression measures how much the ball deforms when hit. Lower numbers = softer balls better for slow swings.

Do premium balls really make a difference?

Yes — for players with consistent swings, premium balls can offer better spin control and feel, especially on approach shots.
